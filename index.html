Current projects

Projects we are currently working on.

Pralinka

The on-line occupancy monitoring system of metro carriages will use simple signalling to recommend where passengers in the follow-up station should stand to get on the least occupied carriages. Occupancy will therefore be distributed uniformly in the whole train, which will increase the travel comfort of passengers. In addition, it will speed up boarding in the busiest stations, which will prevent delays - passengers will save time and the traffic will become smoother.

Past projects

Projects we did in the past.

(( LoRaTor ))

A smart IoT GPS locator that can last for ages on battery. The device is connected LoRaWAN network. The prototype was built on Bosch Connected World 2017 IoT hackathon using Gimasi Tuino development board.

(( PostDog ))

IoT device for monitoring post boxes. This project was focused on Czech Post and aimed at optimalization of logistics during everyday collection of letters from post boxes. It is connected to LoRaWAN network that allows long operation on a battery.

Achievements

DB Hackathon 2017

Invitation to Japan to cooperate with JR East

JR East provided us with their dataset from Yamanote line in Tokyo. The dataset contained occupancy information from every train on the line. We fed the data into our occupancy signalization system presented on CEE Hacks 2017 and built a prediction model with splendid success rate.

CEE Hacks 2017

1st place
4 sponsor prices

The Hackathon was a birthplace for Pralinka project. We presented a solution to uncomfortable and slow subway travelling. Our system provides the passengers on the platform with an information about the fullness of each carriage before arrival of the train. This saves both money and nerves.

CRA Hackathon 2016

2nd place

The organiser was amused by our clever IoT solution for Czech Post. We developed LoRaWAN based device to track post boxes maintained by post companies. The motivation was to create a system for smart collection of the post. The success in the hackathon lead to the PostDog project.

Valeo innovation sprint 2016

1st place

Hackathon focused mainly on computer vision. The task was to create a real-time algorithm to detect lights that appear to blink in the camera but are not actually blinking in real life. This is a common problem with widely spread LED lights. We designed a reliable FFT based solution.
